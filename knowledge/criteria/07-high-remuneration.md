# Criterion 7: High Salary / Remuneration

## Legal Standard
Evidence that the beneficiary has commanded or will command a high salary or other significantly high remuneration, in relation to others in the field. The key phrase is "in relation to others" — compensation must be high RELATIVE to peers in the same field, role, and geography.

## Evidence Hierarchy (strongest to weakest)
1. Offer letter + equity agreement showing total compensation
2. W-2s or tax returns showing actual earned income
3. BLS Occupational Employment Statistics for same SOC code + geography
4. Salary comparison data from Salary.com, Glassdoor, Payscale, Levels.fyi
5. Stock purchase agreement with valuation basis
6. Industry articles on compensation norms

## Startup Founder Equity Argument
The most common pattern for tech entrepreneur O-1As. Required elements:

1. **Salary:** annual amount from offer letter
2. **Equity:** ownership percentage from stock purchase agreement or cap table
3. **Valuation basis:** company valuation from most recent funding round or post-money valuation cap (cite SAFE/term sheet)
4. **Equity value calculation:** ownership % x valuation = equity value
5. **Total compensation:** salary + equity value
6. **USCIS Policy Alert PA-2022-03 citation:** explicitly acknowledges equity as comparable evidence when traditional high salary is not applicable to an entrepreneur
7. **Comparative data:** BLS median for same role + geography
8. **Context:** evidence that low salary + high equity is standard for startup founders

## Best Practices
- Equity ownership alone is NOT sufficient — must include comparative data showing total compensation is high relative to peers
- BLS data alone is insufficient — supplement with Salary.com, Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, or industry compensation surveys
- Always cite USCIS Policy Alert PA-2022-03 (Jan 21, 2022) for equity-based arguments — it is the key policy document
- If cash salary is below market, preempt by acknowledging it and pivoting to equity per PA-2022-03
- SAFE valuation caps are not formal 409A valuations — be prepared to explain why the cap reflects real value
- Submit at least two independent sources of comparative compensation data
- Include W-2s, 1099s, or foreign tax documents showing actual earnings for past employment claims
- The goal is to show total compensation is significantly above the median, not just above it
