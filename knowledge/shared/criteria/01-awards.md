# Criterion 1: Awards / Prizes for Excellence

## Legal Standard
The beneficiary must have received nationally or internationally recognized prizes or awards for excellence in the field. Three sub-elements: (1) receipt by the beneficiary, (2) national/international recognition, (3) awarded for excellence/merit, not participation.

## Evidence Hierarchy (strongest to weakest)
1. Expert letter from award grantor explaining selection process, applicant count, criteria
2. Award certificate/letter with beneficiary individually named
3. News coverage of the award with circulation figures
4. Award organization's website showing criteria, jury, past winners
5. Scholarly article on the evaluation process (for VC-as-award arguments)
6. USCIS precedent showing same award type accepted before
7. Award certificate addressed to a company (must tie beneficiary individually)
8. Screenshots of online recognition (Product Hunt, etc.) — corroboration only

## Winning Argument Patterns

### VC Funding as Award
Required elements:
- Frame each funding round as competitive selection: cite firm's acceptance rate or deal flow vs. investments made
- Cite scholarly research on VC decision-making (e.g., Gompers et al., 2020, Journal of Financial Economics)
- Cite USCIS precedent or policy guidance accepting VC as awards
- Per VC firm: AUM, portfolio size, notable companies, founding year — all sourced
- Emphasize VC evaluated the entrepreneur's ability, not just the company

Argument structure:
1. Beneficiary received $X from Firm — cite SAFE/term sheet exhibit
2. Firm is internationally recognized — cite AUM, portfolio, countries
3. Firm's selection constitutes an award for excellence — cite acceptance rate, scholarly article
4. USCIS has accepted VC funding as awards — cite policy alert or prior approval

### Design/Industry Awards
- Cite number of submissions vs. winners
- Cite jury composition (named experts)
- Cite countries represented in submissions
- If award is to a company, tie beneficiary to the credited team

### Product Hunt / Online Platform Recognition
- Weaker standalone, useful as corroboration
- Frame as peer-reviewed by industry practitioners
- Cite total products launched that day/week vs. ranking achieved
- Include user review count and rating

## Best Practices
- Always submit the actual award certificate, not just a list mention
- Student/academic awards are generally rejected — they exclude established professionals
- For each award: cite number of applicants, winners, countries, jury composition
- If award went to a company, include team credits naming the beneficiary individually
- VC-as-award requires: firm's acceptance rate, scholarly article on VC decision-making, USCIS precedent
- Being on a "list" (even elite) is not the same as receiving an award — USCIS wants the actual award document
- If only awards are from student era, consider dropping this criterion entirely
- Lead with the strongest award; list others briefly as corroboration — don't pad with obscure awards
- Include geographic reach and news coverage of the award itself to prove "nationally/internationally recognized"
