# Criterion 4: Original Contributions of Major Significance

## Legal Standard
"Evidence of the beneficiary's original scientific, scholarly, or business-related contributions of major significance in the field."

### Two Sub-Elements (both must be addressed)
1. **Original** — the contribution is novel, not a replication of existing work
2. **Major significance** — it has had meaningful impact in the field (not just "exists")

This is often the hardest criterion to prove because "major significance" is subjective.

## Evidence Hierarchy (strongest → weakest)

1. **Patent granted** with evidence of industry adoption or licensing
2. **Expert testimonial letters** from independent experts (not co-founders or colleagues) describing the contribution's significance and impact on the field
3. **Quantified adoption metrics** — user count, downloads, revenue, companies using the technology
4. **VC funding received** as a result of the contribution (with amounts and firm names)
5. **Third-party reviews/recognition** — Product Hunt awards, app store ratings, industry awards
6. **Media coverage** of the contribution with circulation figures
7. **Product documentation** — pitch deck, technical architecture, code (proves originality but not significance)
8. **Internal metrics** without third-party validation (weakest — self-reported)

## The Originality + Significance Framework

| | Originality Strong | Originality Weak |
|---|---|---|
| **Significance Strong** | Best case: novel technology with proven adoption and expert validation | Incremental improvement on existing tech, but widely adopted — still arguable |
| **Significance Weak** | Novel concept but no proof anyone uses it — this gets RFE'd | Not arguable — don't use this criterion |

## Proving "Major Significance" — What Actually Works

Adjudicators need to see that the contribution matters BEYOND the beneficiary's own company:

| Evidence Type | Why It Works |
|--------------|-------------|
| Other companies/researchers using the contribution | Proves field-wide impact |
| Citations in academic papers | Proves scholarly recognition |
| Revenue/users at scale | Proves commercial significance |
| Industry awards recognizing the contribution | Third-party validation |
| VC funding at significant valuation | Market says it's valuable |
| Expert letters from people OUTSIDE the beneficiary's org | Independent validation |
| Media coverage describing the contribution as innovative | Third-party characterization |

## Things to Watch For

> These are structural weaknesses to think about when building the argument — not confirmed RFE patterns based on real case data.

| Trigger | Why It Gets RFE'd | How to Prevent |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| No evidence of impact beyond the beneficiary's own company | "The contribution may be original but has not been shown to be of major significance in the field" | Include adoption metrics, third-party reviews, expert letters from outsiders |
| Expert letters are from co-founders or close colleagues | Self-serving — adjudicator discounts these | Get letters from independent experts with no business relationship |
| No user metrics or adoption data | Can't assess significance without numbers | Include exact user count, download count, revenue — with dates |
| Product is described but not compared to alternatives | How is this different from competitors? | Explicitly state what existed before and how this contribution is novel |
| "Major significance" is asserted, not proved | Saying "this is significant" doesn't make it so | Let the evidence speak: metrics, expert quotes, awards, press |
