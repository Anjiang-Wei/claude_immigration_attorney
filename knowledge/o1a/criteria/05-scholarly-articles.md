# Criterion 5: Authorship of Scholarly Articles

## Legal Standard
"Evidence of the beneficiary's authorship of scholarly articles in the field, in professional or major trade publications or other major media."

### Key Distinction from Criterion 3
- Criterion 3: Material written ABOUT the beneficiary by others
- Criterion 5: Articles written BY the beneficiary

Both can be used in the same petition — they address different things.

## Evidence Hierarchy (strongest → weakest)

1. **Peer-reviewed journal article** with DOI, impact factor, and citation count
2. **Article in a major professional publication** (e.g., Forbes, IEEE Spectrum, Harvard Business Review) — editorial process required
3. **Conference proceedings** (peer-reviewed, published by ACM/IEEE/etc.)
4. **arXiv / preprint server papers** (weaker — not peer-reviewed, but shows scholarly activity)
5. **Forbes Council / LinkedIn articles** — technically "published in major media" but weaker because the editorial bar is lower
6. **Blog posts** (generally not accepted)

## Required Sourced Facts Per Article

| Fact | Why It Matters | Where to Find |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| Full citation (authors, title, journal, year, DOI) | Proves it's a real publication | The article itself |
| Journal impact factor | Proves the journal is significant | Journal Citation Reports, Scimago |
| Whether peer-reviewed | Peer review = expert validation | Journal website "About" page |
| Citation count | Proves others read and used it | Google Scholar, Semantic Scholar |
| Journal circulation/readership | Proves major media | SimilarWeb, journal press kit |
| Co-authors and affiliations | Shows collaborative scholarly work | The article itself |

## Things to Watch For

> These are structural weaknesses to think about when building the argument — not confirmed RFE patterns based on real case data.

| Trigger | Why It Gets RFE'd | How to Prevent |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| Article is in a Forbes Council (contributor) section, not main Forbes editorial | Adjudicator views contributor posts as lower editorial standard | Acknowledge the distinction but argue Forbes Business Council requires vetting + editorial review; include the article's reach metrics |
| No impact factor or citation count | Can't assess the article's significance | Always include journal ranking + citation count from Google Scholar |
| Beneficiary is a minor co-author | "Did the beneficiary actually write this?" | Specify the beneficiary's contribution; if first or corresponding author, state that |
| Article topic doesn't relate to the field of endeavor | An article about education theory ≠ "Technology Entrepreneurship" | Explain the connection between the article's topic and the beneficiary's field |
