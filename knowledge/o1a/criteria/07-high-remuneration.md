# Criterion 7: High Salary / Remuneration

## Legal Standard
"Evidence that the beneficiary has either commanded a high salary or will command a high salary or other significantly high remuneration for services, in relation to others in the field."

### Key: "In Relation to Others in the Field"
The salary must be high RELATIVE to peers in the same field, same role, same geography. Context matters.

## Evidence Hierarchy (strongest → weakest)

1. **Offer letter + equity agreement** showing total compensation (salary + equity value)
2. **BLS Occupational Employment Statistics** for the same SOC code + geography
3. **Salary comparison data** from Salary.com, Glassdoor, Payscale, Levels.fyi
4. **USCIS Policy Alert PA-2022-03** (Jan 21, 2022) — establishes equity as comparable evidence
5. **Stock purchase agreement** with valuation basis
6. **Tax returns / W-2s** showing actual earned income (for past employment)
7. **Industry articles** on startup founder compensation norms

## The Startup Founder Equity Argument

This is the most common pattern for tech entrepreneur O-1As. The salary is often low but the equity is substantial.

### Required elements:
1. **Salary:** $[amount]/year — cite offer letter
2. **Equity:** [X]% ownership — cite stock purchase agreement or cap table
3. **Valuation basis:** Company valued at $[Y] based on most recent funding round or post-money valuation cap — cite SAFE or term sheet
4. **Equity value calculation:** [X]% × $[Y] = $[Z]
5. **Total compensation:** Salary + Equity Value = $[total]
6. **USCIS Policy Alert PA-2022-03 citation:**
   > "As another example, if the petitioner demonstrates that receipt of a high salary is not readily applicable to the beneficiary's position as an entrepreneur, the petitioner might present evidence that the beneficiary's highly valued equity holdings in the startup are of comparable significance to the high salary criterion."
7. **Comparative data:** BLS median for the same role + geography
8. **Context articles:** Evidence that low salary + high equity is standard for startup founders

## Things to Watch For

These are areas where this criterion can be vulnerable — not confirmed RFE language, but structural weaknesses to think about when building the argument:

- **Salary alone is below market** — If the cash salary is low, preempt by acknowledging it and pivoting to equity per PA-2022-03
- **Equity valuation basis is soft** — SAFE valuation caps are not formal 409A valuations. Be prepared to explain why the cap reflects real value (third-party investor committed real money at that number)
- **Equity is unvested** — The total compensation package (including unvested equity) is standard in startup compensation, but explain this explicitly
- **Company has minimal revenue** — If the petitioning company is pre-revenue, the equity valuation argument relies entirely on VC investment as third-party validation
- **Multiple SAFEs at different caps** — If the company raised at different valuations across rounds, use the most defensible and explain which one applies
- **No comparative data** — Always include BLS + at least one other salary source for the same role and geography

## Key Policy Reference

**USCIS Policy Alert PA-2022-03 (January 21, 2022):**
This is the single most important policy document for startup founder high-remuneration arguments. It explicitly acknowledges that equity can serve as comparable evidence when a traditional high salary is not applicable. Always cite it. Always include the full exhibit.
