# O-1A Common Weaknesses & RFE Log

Cross-criterion structural weaknesses that can make petitions vulnerable. The "themes" below are based on general immigration practice knowledge — not confirmed RFE data from our cases. Add real RFE entries as they come in.

## Common Weakness Themes

### 1. "Not shown to be nationally or internationally recognized"
**Criteria affected:** Awards, Membership, Published Material
**Root cause:** No quantitative evidence of recognition (no circulation figures, no acceptance rates, no geographic reach data)
**Prevention:** Always include numbers — acceptance rate, circulation, countries represented

### 2. "Not shown to be of major significance"
**Criteria affected:** Original Contributions
**Root cause:** Contribution exists but no proof it matters to the field
**Prevention:** Independent expert letters, adoption metrics, media coverage, VC backing — all from sources outside the beneficiary's own company

### 3. "Not shown to be a critical or essential capacity"
**Criteria affected:** Critical Employment
**Root cause:** Job duties sound like any employee in that role
**Prevention:** Specific, named contributions with measurable outcomes; letters from senior leaders emphasizing what would have been lost without the beneficiary

### 4. "Salary does not appear to be high in relation to others in the field"
**Criteria affected:** High Remuneration
**Root cause:** Low cash salary without proper equity framing
**Prevention:** Cite PA-2022-03; include equity valuation math; include comparative data from BLS + other sources

### 5. "Not shown that the association requires outstanding achievements"
**Criteria affected:** Membership
**Root cause:** No evidence of selectivity — just proof of membership
**Prevention:** Acceptance rate or applicant:accepted ratio; published selection criteria; expert judge profiles

## RFE Response Best Practices

1. **Address the exact language of the RFE** — don't rephrase, don't ignore parts of it
2. **Add new evidence** — an RFE response with only argument and no new evidence usually fails
3. **Don't just repeat what was in the original petition** — the adjudicator already read it and wasn't convinced
4. **Request additional expert letters** that specifically address the RFE's concern
5. **Add web source printouts** for any statistics that were previously unsourced
